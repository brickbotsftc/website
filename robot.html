Robot Components

Breaking down the key systems and mechanisms

Intake

Designed to rapidly and reliably collect game artifacts while maintaining consistent ground contact across uneven surfaces. After numerous design iterations focusing on suspension geometry, roller materials, and mounting configurations, the team developed a bungee-based suspension system that allows the intake to float and conform to the playing surface, ensuring reliable pickup performance while minimizing missed artifacts.

  • Type: Bungee Suspension Roller Intake
  • Drive System: Chain-Driven Power Transmission
  • Gear Ratio: 3:1 Speed Increase
  • Motor Speed: 312 RPM

Outtake

A fully redesigned turret-based shooter built for accuracy, adaptability, and rapid scoring from multiple field locations. The system combines a rotating turret platform mounted on an AndyMark bearing, adjustable hood geometry, and dual Rhino flywheels. Through iterative testing of wheel compression, hood angles, and shot profiles, the team achieved consistent launches across varying distances with vision-based targeting.

  • Type: 360° Rotating Turret Shooter
  • Turret Base: AndyMark Turntable Bearing
  • Flywheels: Dual Rhino Motors
  • Compression: 6.5mm Tuned
  • Vision System: Limelight 3A Auto-Targeting

Transfer

After extensive prototyping and moving away from the initial spindexer concept, BrickBots developed a robust multi-stage wheel conveyor system. Through multiple design iterations, geometry changes, and material tests, the team created a solution that balances speed, control, and consistency while minimizing jams and maximizing cycle time.

  • Drive System: Chain-Driven from Intake
  • Stage 1 (Acceleration): 96mm Hogback Wheels (3x speed)
  • Stages 2-3 (Control): Modified 81mm TPU Swift Wheels
  • Final Stage: Kicker Arm to Turret
